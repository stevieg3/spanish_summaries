Present Tense - Irregular, Reflexive, and Stem-Changing Verbs

Irregular Verbs in Present Tense

While many Spanish verbs follow regular conjugation patterns, some of the most commonly used verbs are irregular. These verbs do not follow the standard rules and must be memorized.

Subject ser (to be) ir (to go) tener (to have) hacer (to do/make)
yo soy voy tengo hago
tú eres vas tienes haces
él/ella/usted es va tiene hace
nosotros/nosotras somos vamos tenemos hacemos
vosotros/vosotras sois vais tenéis hacéis
ellos/ellas/ustedes son van tienen hacen
Subject decir (to say) venir (to come) ver (to see) dar (to give)
yo digo vengo veo doy
tú dices vienes ves das
él/ella/usted dice viene ve da
nosotros/nosotras decimos venimos vemos damos
vosotros/vosotras decís venís veis dais
ellos/ellas/ustedes dicen vienen ven dan

Examples:

Yo soy de España. (I am from Spain.)

Él va al supermercado. (He goes to the supermarket.)

Nosotros tenemos dos gatos. (We have two cats.)

Ellos hacen la tarea. (They do the homework.)

¿Vienes a la fiesta? (Are you coming to the party?)

Reflexive Verbs

Reflexive verbs are used when the subject performs an action on itself. These verbs are identified by the reflexive pronoun "se" attached to the end of the infinitive form (e.g., lavarse = to wash oneself).

Reflexive Pronouns

Subject Reflexive Pronoun
yo me
tú te
él/ella/usted se
nosotros/nosotras nos
vosotros/vosotras os
ellos/ellas/ustedes se

Conjugation of Reflexive Verbs

To conjugate reflexive verbs, place the reflexive pronoun before the conjugated form of the verb:

Subject Reflexive Pronoun lavarse (to wash oneself)
yo me me lavo
tú te te lavas
él/ella/usted se se lava
nosotros/nosotras nos nos lavamos
vosotros/vosotras os os laváis
ellos/ellas/ustedes se se lavan

Common reflexive verbs:

lavarse (to wash oneself)

peinarse (to comb one's hair)

vestirse (to get dressed)

ducharse (to shower)

despertarse (to wake up)

acostarse (to go to bed)

levantarse (to get up)

Examples:

Me lavo las manos antes de comer. (I wash my hands before eating.)

¿A qué hora te despiertas? (At what time do you wake up?)

Ella se peina por la mañana. (She combs her hair in the morning.)

Nos acostamos a las diez. (We go to bed at ten.)

Los niños se duchan después de jugar. (The children shower after playing.)

Note: Some verbs change meaning when used in their reflexive form:

Dormir (to sleep) vs. Dormirse (to fall asleep)

Ir (to go) vs. Irse (to leave, go away)

Llamar (to call) vs. Llamarse (to be called, named)

Stem-Changing Verbs

Stem-changing verbs undergo a vowel change in the stem (root) of the verb in certain conjugations. The endings follow regular patterns, but the stem changes. There are three main types of stem changes:

E → IE Stem Changes

Subject pensar (to think) querer (to want)
yo pienso quiero
tú piensas quieres
él/ella/usted piensa quiere
nosotros/nosotras pensamos queremos
vosotros/vosotras pensáis queréis
ellos/ellas/ustedes piensan quieren

Other common e → ie verbs:

comenzar (to begin)

empezar (to start)

entender (to understand)

preferir (to prefer)

cerrar (to close)

O → UE Stem Changes

Subject poder (to be able) dormir (to sleep)
yo puedo duermo
tú puedes duermes
él/ella/usted puede duerme
nosotros/nosotras podemos dormimos
vosotros/vosotras podéis dormís
ellos/ellas/ustedes pueden duermen

Other common o → ue verbs:

contar (to count, tell)

encontrar (to find)

volver (to return)

recordar (to remember)

mostrar (to show)

E → I Stem Changes

Subject pedir (to ask for) servir (to serve)
yo pido sirvo
tú pides sirves
él/ella/usted pide sirve
nosotros/nosotras pedimos servimos
vosotros/vosotras pedís servís
ellos/ellas/ustedes piden sirven

Other common e → i verbs:

repetir (to repeat)

seguir (to follow, continue)

vestir (to dress)

elegir (to choose)

corregir (to correct)

Note: The stem change only occurs in the singular forms and in the third person plural (ellos/ellas/ustedes). The nosotros/as and vosotros/as forms maintain the original vowel.

Examples:

Yo pienso que es una buena idea. (I think it's a good idea.)

¿Puedes ayudarme? (Can you help me?)

Ella sirve la cena a las ocho. (She serves dinner at eight.)

Nosotros queremos ir al cine. (We want to go to the movies.)

Los estudiantes entienden la lección. (The students understand the lesson.)

Conjugation of "gustar" and Similar Verbs

"Gustar" and similar verbs work differently in Spanish than in English. In English, we say "I like something," but in Spanish, the structure is more like "Something pleases me" (Me gusta algo).

Structure of "gustar"

Subject (English) Indirect Object Pronoun Verb Form Spanish Sentence
I me gusta/gustan Me gusta el libro. (I like the book.)
you (informal) te gusta/gustan Te gusta la música. (You like music.)
he/she/it/you (formal) le gusta/gustan Le gusta bailar. (He/she likes to dance.)
we nos gusta/gustan Nos gusta el restaurante. (We like the restaurant.)
you all (informal) os gusta/gustan Os gusta la película. (You all like the movie.)
they/you all (formal) les gusta/gustan Les gusta viajar. (They like to travel.)

Note: The form of "gustar" depends on what is being liked:

- Use gusta (singular) with:

  • Singular nouns: Me gusta el libro. (I like the book.)
  • Infinitive verbs: Nos gusta caminar. (We like to walk.)

- Use gustan (plural) with:

  • Plural nouns: Te gustan los gatos. (You like cats.)

Clarifying the Subject

To specify who likes something, use "a + person" after the verb:

Le gusta el fútbol a Juan. (Juan likes soccer.)

Les gustan las películas de acción a mis amigos. (My friends like action movies.)

Similar Verbs

Several other verbs work like "gustar," following the same structure with indirect object pronouns:

Verb Meaning Example
encantar to love, to really like Me encanta esa canción. (I love that song.)
interesar to interest Le interesa la política. (He is interested in politics.)
importar to matter, to care No me importa el dinero. (Money doesn't matter to me.)
faltar to lack, to need Nos falta tiempo. (We need more time/We lack time.)
doler to hurt Me duele la cabeza. (My head hurts.)
parecer to seem ¿Te parece buena idea? (Does it seem like a good idea to you?)
quedar to fit, to suit, to remain Te queda bien ese vestido. (That dress suits you well.)

More examples:

Nos encanta ir a la playa. (We love going to the beach.)

Le interesan los libros de historia. (He is interested in history books.)

No les importan las críticas. (They don't care about the criticism.)

Me duelen las piernas. (My legs hurt.)

¿Te parece difícil el español? (Does Spanish seem difficult to you?)

Quantifiers

Quantifiers express quantity or amount in Spanish. They must agree in gender and number with the nouns they modify.

Common Quantifiers

English Masculine Singular Feminine Singular Masculine Plural Feminine Plural
much, many mucho mucha muchos muchas
little, few poco poca pocos pocas
some, a few algún alguna algunos algunas
enough, quite a few bastante bastante bastantes bastantes
no, none ningún ninguna ningunos* ningunas*
several — — varios varias
too much, too many demasiado demasiada demasiados demasiadas

* "Ningunos" and "ningunas" are rarely used in plural form. Spanish typically uses "no... ningunos/ningunas" for emphasis.

Usage of Quantifiers

Examples:

Tengo muchos libros pero pocos amigos. (I have many books but few friends.)

Hay demasiada gente y bastante ruido. (There are too many people and quite a lot of noise.)

No tengo ningún problema con algunas de tus ideas. (I don't have any problem with some of your ideas.)

Hay varios restaurantes buenos en esta calle. (There are several good restaurants on this street.)

¿Tienes mucho trabajo hoy? (Do you have a lot of work today?)

Necesitamos bastantes voluntarios para el evento. (We need quite a few volunteers for the event.)

Note: "Algún" and "ningún" drop the final "o" before masculine singular nouns, but keep it in other forms:

Algún libro (Some book) but Algunos libros (Some books)

Ningún problema (No problem) but Ninguna idea (No idea)

Frequency Expressions

Frequency expressions indicate how often an action occurs. In Spanish, they are typically placed:

  • After the verb
  • Before the verb
  • At the beginning or end of the sentence (followed by a comma when at the beginning)

Common Frequency Expressions

Spanish English Example
nunca / jamás never Ella nunca llega tarde. (She never arrives late.)
casi nunca almost never, hardly ever Casi nunca veo la televisión. (I almost never watch television.)
rara vez / raramente rarely Rara vez como carne. (I rarely eat meat.)
a veces sometimes A veces, vamos al cine. (Sometimes, we go to the movies.)
una vez a la semana once a week Voy al gimnasio una vez a la semana. (I go to the gym once a week.)
dos veces al mes twice a month Limpio mi casa dos veces al mes. (I clean my house twice a month.)
cada semana every week Vamos al cine cada semana. (We go to the movies every week.)
todos los días every day Estudio español todos los días. (I study Spanish every day.)
a menudo often Visito a menudo a mis abuelos. (I often visit my grandparents.)
con frecuencia / frecuentemente frequently, often Ellos viajan con frecuencia. (They travel frequently.)
normalmente / por lo general normally, usually Normalmente, estudio por la noche. (Normally, I study at night.)
generalmente generally Generalmente, hace calor en verano. (Generally, it's hot in summer.)
casi siempre almost always Casi siempre llega a tiempo. (He almost always arrives on time.)
siempre always Yo siempre desayuno a las ocho. (I always have breakfast at eight.)

More examples:

Siempre desayuno frutas. (I always eat fruit for breakfast.)

Nunca bebo café por la noche. (I never drink coffee at night.)

A veces vamos a la playa los domingos. (Sometimes we go to the beach on Sundays.)

Mis padres nos visitan una vez al mes. (My parents visit us once a month.)

¿Practicas deporte con frecuencia? (Do you practice sports frequently?)

Yo casi siempre leo antes de dormir. (I almost always read before sleeping.)

Raramente salgo durante la semana. (I rarely go out during the week.)

Note: When using "nunca" or "jamás," you don't need the word "no" in the sentence, as they are already negative:

Correct: Nunca voy al cine. (I never go to the movies.)

Incorrect: No nunca voy al cine.
