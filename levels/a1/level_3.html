Level 3 (A1)

Pretérito Indefinido (Simple Past)

The Pretérito Indefinido (also called Pretérito Perfecto Simple) is used to express completed actions in the past. Unlike the Imperfect tense, which describes ongoing or habitual past actions, the Indefinido focuses on actions that started and ended at a specific point in time.

When to Use the Pretérito Indefinido

  • Completed actions at a specific time in the past: "Yesterday I bought a book" (Ayer compré un libro)
  • Actions that happened a specific number of times: "I visited Spain twice" (Visité España dos veces)
  • Actions that interrupted another ongoing action: "I was reading when he arrived" (Estaba leyendo cuando él llegó)
  • Series of completed actions: "I got up, got dressed, and left" (Me levanté, me vestí y salí)

Regular Conjugations

Subject -AR verbs -ER/-IR verbs
yo hablé comí
tú hablaste comiste
él/ella/usted habló comió
nosotros/as hablamos comimos
vosotros/as hablasteis comisteis
ellos/ellas/ustedes hablaron comieron

Note: The accents in the yo, él/ella/usted forms are essential as they change the stress of the word and distinguish these forms from other tenses.

Common Time Expressions with Pretérito Indefinido

  • ayer (yesterday)
  • anteayer (the day before yesterday)
  • la semana pasada (last week)
  • el mes pasado (last month)
  • el año pasado (last year)
  • hace dos días/semanas/meses/años (two days/weeks/months/years ago)
  • anoche (last night)
  • una vez (one time, once)

Irregular Verbs in the Pretérito Indefinido

Many common Spanish verbs have irregular forms in the Pretérito Indefinido. There are several types of irregularities:

1. Completely Irregular Verbs

These verbs have unique stems and endings:

Subject ser/ir (to be/to go) dar (to give) ver (to see)
yo fui di vi
tú fuiste diste viste
él/ella/usted fue dio vio
nosotros/as fuimos dimos vimos
vosotros/as fuisteis disteis visteis
ellos/ellas/ustedes fueron dieron vieron

Note: "Ser" and "ir" have identical conjugations in the Pretérito Indefinido despite having different meanings.

2. Stem-changing Verbs

These verbs have irregular stems but follow a consistent pattern of endings:

Infinitive Irregular Stem
tener (to have) tuv-
estar (to be) estuv-
poder (to be able) pud-
poner (to put) pus-
saber (to know) sup-
hacer (to do/make) hic- (hiz- in 3rd person singular)
venir (to come) vin-
querer (to want) quis-
andar (to walk) anduv-

Most of these verbs use these endings with their irregular stems:

Person Ending Example (tener → tuv-)
yo -e tuve
tú -iste tuviste
él/ella/usted -o tuvo
nosotros/as -imos tuvimos
vosotros/as -isteis tuvisteis
ellos/ellas/ustedes -ieron tuvieron

Note: There are some exceptions to these patterns:

  • "Hacer" uses "hizo" (not "hició") in the third-person singular
  • "Decir" (dij-) and "traer" (traj-) drop the 'i' in the third-person plural: dijeron, trajeron (not dijieron, trajieron)
  • "Producir" (to produce) follows the pattern of "decir": produje, produjiste, produjo, etc.
  • "Conducir" (to drive) also follows this pattern: conduje, condujiste, condujo, etc.

3. Verbs with Spelling Changes

These verbs undergo spelling changes in the first-person singular (yo) form to maintain pronunciation:

Type of Change Example Yo Form
-car → -qué buscar (to look for) busqué
-gar → -gué llegar (to arrive) llegué
-zar → -cé empezar (to begin) empecé
-guar → -güé averiguar (to find out) averigüé

Note: These spelling changes only occur in the first-person singular (yo) form. All other forms follow the regular conjugation pattern (buscaste, llegaste, empezaste, averiguaste, etc.).

4. Stem-changing -IR Verbs

Some -IR verbs that have stem changes in the present tense also have stem changes in the Pretérito Indefinido, but only in the third-person singular and plural forms:

Infinitive Stem Change Él/Ella/Ud. Ellos/Ellas/Uds.
sentir (to feel) e → i sintió sintieron
dormir (to sleep) o → u durmió durmieron

Note: These stem changes only occur in the third-person singular (él/ella/usted) and plural (ellos/ellas/ustedes) forms. Other forms are regular (sentí, sentiste, sentimos, etc.)

Other common -IR verbs with the e → i change include: mentir (to lie), preferir (to prefer), herir (to wound), divertir (to entertain), sugerir (to suggest), and convertir (to convert).

The o → u change is less common and mainly appears in dormir (to sleep) and morir (to die).

5. Verbs with Stems Ending in a Vowel

Verbs with stems ending in a vowel (like leer, caer, oír) add a "y" in the third-person forms:

Subject leer (to read)
yo leí
tú leíste
él/ella/usted leyó
nosotros/as leímos
vosotros/as leísteis
ellos/ellas/ustedes leyeron

Note: The "y" only appears in third-person forms (él/ella/usted and ellos/ellas/ustedes). Other verbs that follow this pattern include:

  • caer (to fall): caí, caíste, cayó, caímos, caísteis, cayeron
  • creer (to believe): creí, creíste, creyó, creímos, creísteis, creyeron
  • oír (to hear): oí, oíste, oyó, oímos, oísteis, oyeron
  • construir (to build): construí, construiste, construyó, construimos, construisteis, construyeron

Examples:

Ayer compré un libro interesante. (Yesterday I bought an interesting book.)

Ellos fueron a Madrid el año pasado. (They went to Madrid last year.)

Hablamos con el profesor después de la clase. (We spoke with the teacher after class.)

¿Viste la película anoche? (Did you see the movie last night?)

María estuvo enferma la semana pasada. (Maria was sick last week.)

Difference between "saber" and "conocer"

Both "saber" and "conocer" translate to "to know" in English, but they are used in different contexts and cannot be used interchangeably.

Saber (to know facts or information)

  • Used for knowing facts, information, or how to do something
  • Used to express knowledge of a skill or ability
  • Used with "que" for factual statements
  • Used with infinitives to express "knowing how to"

Conocer (to be familiar with or acquainted with)

  • Used for knowing or being familiar with people, places, or things
  • Expresses personal acquaintance or familiarity
  • Used when talking about having met someone or visited a place
  • Used for recognizing things or people

Examples of SABER:

Yo sé hablar español. (I know how to speak Spanish.)

¿Sabes qué hora es? (Do you know what time it is?)

Ella sabe la respuesta. (She knows the answer.)

No sabemos cocinar paella. (We don't know how to cook paella.)

Ellos saben que mañana es el examen. (They know that tomorrow is the exam.)

Examples of CONOCER:

Yo conozco a María. (I know Maria. - I've met her.)

¿Conoces Madrid? (Do you know Madrid? - Have you been there?)

Ella conoce bien este restaurante. (She knows this restaurant well. - She's familiar with it.)

No conocemos al profesor nuevo. (We don't know the new teacher. - We haven't met him.)

Ellos conocen muchos países. (They know many countries. - They've visited them.)

Present Tense Conjugation

Subject SABER CONOCER
yo sé conozco
tú sabes conoces
él/ella/usted sabe conoce
nosotros/as sabemos conocemos
vosotros/as sabéis conocéis
ellos/ellas/ustedes saben conocen

Note: "Saber" has an irregular yo form (sé) and "conocer" has a spelling change in the yo form (conozco).

Pretérito Indefinido Conjugation

Subject SABER CONOCER
yo supe conocí
tú supiste conociste
él/ella/usted supo conoció
nosotros/as supimos conocimos
vosotros/as supisteis conocisteis
ellos/ellas/ustedes supieron conocieron

Note: In the past tense, "saber" can also mean "to find out" or "to learn": Ayer supe la verdad (Yesterday I found out the truth).

Important Prepositions

Prepositions are small words that establish relationships between different elements in a sentence. Here are some essential Spanish prepositions and their common uses:

1. "de" (of, from)

  • To express possession: El libro de Juan (Juan's book)
  • To indicate origin: Soy de México (I am from Mexico)
  • To specify material: Un anillo de oro (A gold ring)
  • To express content: Una taza de café (A cup of coffee)
  • After certain verbs: Depender de (to depend on)

La casa de mi abuela está en el campo. (My grandmother's house is in the countryside.)

Este vino es de España. (This wine is from Spain.)

Estoy cansado de trabajar. (I'm tired of working.)

2. "desde" (from, since)

  • To indicate a starting point in space: Desde aquí puedo ver el mar (From here I can see the sea)
  • To indicate a starting point in time: Desde 2010 vivo en Madrid (Since 2010 I have lived in Madrid)

Estudio español desde hace tres años. (I've been studying Spanish for three years.)

Caminamos desde la estación hasta el parque. (We walked from the station to the park.)

3. "hasta" (until, to, up to)

  • To indicate limit in space: Corrí hasta la playa (I ran to the beach)
  • To indicate limit in time: Trabajaré hasta las seis (I will work until six)
  • To express limit in quantity: Puedes gastar hasta 100 euros (You can spend up to 100 euros)

El museo está abierto hasta las ocho. (The museum is open until eight.)

No me dijiste nada hasta hoy. (You didn't tell me anything until today.)

4. "a" (to, at)

  • To indicate direction: Voy a Barcelona (I'm going to Barcelona)
  • To indicate time: Llegamos a las 9 (We arrive at 9)
  • With direct objects that are people: Veo a mi amigo (I see my friend)
  • With many verbs of movement: Llegar a (to arrive at)
  • To express manner: A pie (by foot/on foot)

Voy a estudiar esta noche. (I'm going to study tonight.)

La reunión empieza a las diez. (The meeting starts at ten.)

Viajamos a Francia cada verano. (We travel to France every summer.)

5. "por" (for, through, by, because of)

  • To express cause or reason: Lo hice por ti (I did it for you)
  • To indicate means or "by": Enviar por correo (to send by mail)
  • To express movement through a space: Caminar por el parque (to walk through the park)
  • To express exchange: ¿Cuánto pagaste por el libro? (How much did you pay for the book?)
  • To express approximate time: Por la tarde (in the afternoon)
  • To express gratitude: Gracias por todo (Thanks for everything)

Pasamos por el centro de la ciudad. (We passed through the city center.)

Lo hice por ayudarte. (I did it to help you.)

Te pagué 20 euros por el libro. (I paid you 20 euros for the book.)

6. "en" (in, on, at)

  • To indicate location: Vivo en Madrid (I live in Madrid)
  • To indicate time when something happens: En verano (in summer)
  • To indicate means of transportation: Viajar en tren (to travel by train)
  • To indicate the state of something: En español (in Spanish)

Mi familia vive en Colombia. (My family lives in Colombia.)

Siempre voy al trabajo en autobús. (I always go to work by bus.)

El examen es en diciembre. (The exam is in December.)

Note: Many verbs in Spanish require specific prepositions that may be different from their English equivalents. It's important to learn these verb-preposition combinations:

Pensar en (to think about/of)

Soñar con (to dream about/of)

Hablar de (to talk about)
